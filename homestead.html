Texas Homestead Exemptions

Last Updated September 5, 2021

Eligible homeowners can save money every year by filing a homestead exemption. It costs nothing to file, and stays in place as long as you own your home.

Collin County

Collin County homeowners can download a homestead exemption application here.

Or, you can call the Appraisal District at (469) 742-9200 and they will mail you a form.

More information can be found on the Appraisal District's website.

Denton County

Denton County homeowners can download a homestead exemption application here.

Or, you can call the Appraisal District at (940) 349-3800 and they will mail you a form.

More information can be found on the Appraisal District's website.

Tarrant County

Tarrant County homeowners can download a homestead exemption application here.

Or, you can call the Appraisal District at (817) 284-0024 and they will mail you a form.

More information can be found on the Appraisal District's website.

Steps to Filing Your Homestead Exemption

It'll be done before you know it!

1

Download the Form

The application form can be downloaded from your appraisal district's web site, or directly from the State Comptroller's office here.

Links to several local appraisal district web sites are included above

2

File the Form

Fill out the form and mail it to your appraisal district with the required documentation. This is typically a copy of your driver's license or state ID - the address must match the address of the property you are claiming as your homestead, with limited exceptions.

3

Enjoy the Savings!

You only have to file the form once to enjoy the tax savings year after year. If you become eligible for other exemptions in the future (for example, once you turn 65) you would file for the relevant exemption at that time.

Please contact me if you have questions about the Homestead Exemption, or need help filling out the form. This is a completely free process designed to save you money!
