Texas Homestead Exemptions

A do-it-yourself guide by Bart McLeroy
Originally published August 28, 2021

Eligible homeowners can save money every year by filing a homestead exemption. It costs nothing to file, and stays in place as long as you own your house.

Texas homeowners are legally entitled to a reduction in property taxes for their primary residence. Don't pay more than you need to in property taxes!

Texas law states that property owners who file a homestead exemption with their local Appraisal District get at least a $25,000 exemption on the taxable value of their home for taxes levied by their local school district. So if your property is assessed at $100,000, once you file a homestead exemption you would be taxed by the school district as though it were worth $75,000. Other taxing entities like cities have the option to offer homestead exemptions of up to 20% of the property's total value.

Most property owners in Texas save at least $300 per year in taxes after filing a homestead exemption. Some save more, depending on where their property is located. That's money saved year after year, for having filed paperwork one time!

You can file this exemption as soon as January 1 the year after you buy your home. If you've owned your home for a while and never filed, it's not too late!

Filing a homestead exemption has other benefits. The assessed value of a homestead property cannot be increased by more than 10% per year, regardless of market conditons. This can add up to huge savings over time.

It's worth noting that if you make improvements to the property, that could cause an increase in assessed value, even for homestead properties.

There are other property tax exemptions that you can apply for, including homeowners who are 65 or older, disabled persons, and disabled veterans. See your county appraisal district's website for more information. Some links are listed below, or just do a web search for "(your county) Appraisal District".

Steps to Filing Your Homestead Exemption

It'll be done before you know it!

1

Download the Form

The application form can be downloaded from your appraisal district's web site, or directly from the State Comptroller's office here.

Links to several local appraisal district web sites are included below.

2

File the Form

Fill out the form and submit it to your appraisal district with the required documentation. This is typically a copy of your driver's license or state ID - the address must match the address of the property you are claiming as your homestead, with limited exceptions.

3

Enjoy the Savings!

You only have to file the form once to enjoy the tax savings year after year. If you become eligible for other exemptions in the future (for example, once you turn 65) you would file for the relevant exemption at that time.
